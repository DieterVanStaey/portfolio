Introduction

After 20 years of working as an uneducated worker in all kinds of industries, I decided it was time to get a degree. But what kind of degree? I like to work with audio, more specifically sounddesign and experimental soundprogramming with visual audioprogramming software like Pure Data or Max/MSP. After a while it became clear to me, that the programming part was more interesting and fun to me than the audio part, and I wanted to be able to do more. A choice was made.

September 2019 I started a programmer course at the Artesis Plantijn graduate school. While I'm still in the very beginnings of the course, the motivation to stay in the metal industry is declining rapidly. The plan was to stay for another 3 years, until I finished the course. But at the moment I'm very open to a new, challenging job, and if there's a chance to make the switch earlier, I'd take it with my both hands.

Off course I don't bring a lot of technical experience, but I think I can compensate that with my enthousiasm, fast learning and curiosity. I'm looking for entry level jobs, where I can gain knowledge and experience, but where I'm also able to grow. Being surrounded by creative people inspires me so at the moment I'm looking for a (future) carreer in front-end developing.
